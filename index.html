Introduction

During the early 1990s, the rapid adoption of web technologies led to an explosion of innovation. However, the lack of universal standards resulted in browser inconsistencies, security vulnerabilities, and usability issues. Each browser rendered HTML differently, frustrating developers and users. Proprietary technologies further exacerbated fragmentation, prioritizing competition over interoperability.

To address these challenges, Tim Berners-Lee founded the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) in 1994. His vision was to create a unified framework where websites could function consistently across all browsers and devices. Since then, W3C has played a pivotal role in shaping the internet, enforcing standards that promote accessibility, security, and scalability.
